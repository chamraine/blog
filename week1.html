Week 1

By Charmaine Cheng  -  October 20th, 2023.

In Week 1, we were given a challenge statement on data collection applications and our partnered company was AIDC where we will have till week 5 to finish our prototype for BYTE Hackathon.

The challenge statement involved in three different industries such as Service, Manufacturing and Security.

We quickly formed into groups and soon after discussed on our understanding of the challenge statement, my group discussed collectively and contributed by sharing ideas. One of it was to identify the employees of AIDC by using a survelliance camera that had been predictively AI trained to recognise AIDC employees. The AI will then record the employees attendance after going through the database of all the employees' personal details.

Some problem that we encountered as a team was that what can we do about attendance who are working from home.

As a discussed, we brainstormed and came into an agreement to create an online website for attendance taking where employees are able to see their clock in clock out timing, submit work leave or medical leave. Our finalize idea was to train the AI to identify the employees based on a database provided.

In the database, the employees' face and personal details will be recorded down. If possible, one additional feature we would like to attempt is for the AI to be able to detect whether the person is an object or a human. By doing so, it prevents other employees from helping the other employees take fake attendance by using a photo of them.

As for OAL reflection, I finished the online course on becoming a Design Thinking practitioner.

Through this detailed course, my main takeaway was to have a strong focus on user outcomes. It taught us how to accurately identify my users and problems, then recognise assumptions and learn how to observe my users to learn more about them. Not only that even before confirming the challenge statement, I should ask myself the 5 whys. This is so that the challenge statement will take in account of user's perspective.

In a nutshell, I hope to apply what I have learnt in this course to my group's solution to the challenge statements.
